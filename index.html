Lab 1: Sprint Planning - Set the Vision


    //Before we even start work, we need to know where we're going. As the Agile Project Manager - let's guide our team to success. In this lab, you're going to (a) define the product vision, (b) identify and classify stakeholder, (c) write Epics, and (d) write user stories
    Learning Objective: Convert Stakeholder needs into actionable Agile artificats

    Throughout class, we'll use the following scenario for all of our labs. (Don't worry - it's on every page!)
    Project Title: Brosnan-Jeter Prestige Healthcare Worldwide Application Development and Deployment
    Description: An up-and-coming start-up led by two healthcare prodigies, Riley and Kevin, has secured funding to develop Brosnan-Jeter Prestige Healthcare Worldwide. It will serve as a mobile and desktop application that connects patients with their Primary Care Providers for virtual care, ranging from Physical Therapy and Diagnoses to Prescriptions and Testing. The Minimum Viable Product must be delivered in 6 weeks to meet a demo for a major hospital partner. Kevin and Riley and very strict on this 6 week demo – their future funding and business success depends on this.
    Kevin and Riley are results-driven entrepreneurs with a bold vision to modernize patient care through streamlined, intuitive technology. While Kevin focuses on strategic alignment and brand reputation, Riley is more focused on clinical credibility and practical healthcare impact.
    Riley and Kevin have outlined some of their most critical business goals which are listed below, in order of importance:
      1. Reduce in-clinic appointment load by 25%
      2. Enable secure, HIPAA-compliant messaging and video calls between patients and Primary Care Providers
      3. Allow patients to view care plans, medications, and appointment schedules
      4. Deliver a seamless user experience across all devices
    Stakeholders:
     
    Stakeholder Name Role Interests / Priorities
    Kevin Jeter Co-Founder; CEO High level results: timeline, demo success, brand perception and investor confidence
    Riley Brosnan Co-Founder; Chief Clinical Officer Patient outcomes, clinical workflow integration, real-world medical credibility
    Dr. Priya Patel Primary Care Provider; Clinical Advisor Ease of use for providers, real-time patient access, stable video conferencing
    Janelle Ross Chief Product Officer (CPO) Scope control, velocity, feature prioritization – must hit MVP definition
    Daniel Kwon Compliance and Risk Management Lead HIPAA, encryption, secure access control – nothing ships without his compliance sign-off
    Lisa Thompson Patient Advocate Represents users – needs accessibility, plain language, ease of use
    Dev Team (YOU!) Internal Team Members Balancing deliver with code quality, leads sprint planning and backlog management

    Here are some of the Key Requested Features, sourced from all stakeholders:
      1. Secure login and role-based access
      2. Ability to view personal care plan and appointments
      3. Schedule or cancel an appointment with a PCP
      4. Send or receive secure messages between patient and provider
      5. Start, join, and end real-time video consults
      6. Push notifications for reminders
    Here are some of the Requirements and Constraints, sourced from all stakeholders:
      1. Must meet HIPAA compliance standards
      2. Must function on iOS, Android, and Web
      3. Must be accessible for all users, specifically older users and vision-impaired users
      4. Must be demo ready in 6 weeks
    Potential Challenges to think about:
      1. Kevin and Riley have different priorities, namely vision vs. practicality, which may lead to mid-sprint changes
      2. Clinical advisors may request more features that the CPO is comfortable committing to
      3. There is only one Dev Team to work one sprint at a time
      4. Late stage compliance and accessibility concerns have the potential to delay the MVP if not addressed early or managed properly
