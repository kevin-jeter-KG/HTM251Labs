Scenario Details

    Project Title: Brosnan-Jeter Prestige Healthcare Worldwide Application Development and Deployment

    Description: An up-and-coming start-up led by two healthcare prodigies, Riley and Kevin, has secured funding to develop Brosnan-Jeter Prestige Healthcare Worldwide. It will serve as a mobile and desktop application that connects patients with their Primary Care Providers for virtual care, ranging from Physical Therapy and Diagnoses to Prescriptions and Testing. The Minimum Viable Product must be delivered in 6 weeks to meet a demo for a major hospital partner. Kevin and Riley and very strict on this 6 week demo – their future funding and business success depends on this.

    Kevin and Riley are results-driven entrepreneurs with a bold vision to modernize patient care through streamlined, intuitive technology. While Kevin focuses on strategic alignment and brand reputation, Riley is more focused on clinical credibility and practical healthcare impact.

    Outlined below are some of their most critical business goals, in order of importance:
      1. Reduce in-clinic appointment load by 25%
      2. Enable secure, HIPAA-compliant messaging and video calls between patients and Primary Care Providers
      3. Allow patients to view care plans, medications, and appointment schedules
      4. Deliver a seamless user experience across all devices

    Stakeholder Name Role Interests / Priorities
    Kevin Jeter Co-Founder; CEO High level results: timeline, demo success, brand perception and investor confidence
    Riley Brosnan Co-Founder; Chief Clinical Officer Patient outcomes, clinical workflow integration, real-world medical credibility
    Dr. Priya Patel Primary Care Provider; Clinical Advisor Ease of use for providers, real-time patient access, stable video conferencing
    Janelle Ross Chief Product Officer (CPO) Scope control, velocity, feature prioritization – must hit MVP definition
    Daniel Kwon Compliance and Risk Management Lead HIPAA, encryption, secure access control – nothing ships without his compliance sign-off
    Lisa Thompson Patient Advocate Represents users – needs accessibility, plain language, ease of use
    Dev Team (YOU!) Internal Team Members Balancing deliver with code quality, leads sprint planning and backlog management

    Key Requested Features, sourced from all stakeholders, include:
      1. Secure login and role-based access
      2. Ability to view personal care plan and appointments
      3. Schedule or cancel an appointment with a PCP
      4. Send or receive secure messages between patient and provider
      5. Start, join, and end real-time video consults
      6. Push notifications for reminders

    Requirements and Constraints, sourced from all stakeholders, include:
      1. Must meet HIPAA compliance standards
      2. Must function on iOS, Android, and Web
      3. Must be accessible for all users, specifically older users and vision-impaired users
      4. Must be demo ready in 6 weeks

    Potential Challenges include:
      1. Kevin and Riley have different priorities, namely vision vs. practicality, which may lead to mid-sprint changes
      2. Clinical advisors may request more features that the CPO is comfortable committing to
      3. There is only one Dev Team to work one sprint at a time
      4. Late stage compliance and accessibility concerns have the potential to delay the MVP if not addressed early or managed properly

Lab 2: Shaping our Sprints and Tracking Progress

    //In this lab, you'll be creating Epics, writing User Stories, creating a Product Backlog, and prioritizing work items.

    Description: An up-and-coming start-up led by two healthcare prodigies, Riley and Kevin, has secured funding to develop Brosnan-Jeter Prestige Healthcare Worldwide. It will serve as a mobile and desktop application that connects patients with their Primary Care Providers for virtual care, ranging from Physical Therapy and Diagnoses to Prescriptions and Testing.     Outlined below are some of their most critical business goals, in order of importance:
       1. Reduce in-clinic appointment load by 25%

       3. Allow patients to view care plans, medications, and appointment schedules

  1. Based on the goals, stakeholder needs, and requested features, identify 3 Epics that represent the major feature groups or outcomes for the MVP.

    Creating Epics:

    1. An Epic should be broad enough to include 3–7 user stories, but not so vague it loses focus.
    2. If you find a cluster of related user stories, group them under a single Epic. Think of an Epic as a container that makes backlog management more manageable and thematic.
      If several stories start with “As a patient, I want to…” related to communication, they likely belong to an Epic about messaging or interaction.
    3. It's ok to start with User Stories first and then come back to catergorize you User Stories into Epics. Agile isn't prescriptive - it's what works best for YOU.


    Epic Title Description Primary Stakeholders Impacted
    Virtual Consultations Enable secure, HIPAA-compliant real-time video calls between patients and PCPs Riley Brosnan, Dr. Patel, Daniel Kwon

  2. For the Epics you created, write 4 User Stories. Ensure your stories are meeting the INVEST criteria!

    Creating User Stories:

    "As a [user type], I want to [action or goal] so that [benefit or reason]"

     Hints for writing:
      1. Avoid generic phrases like "As a user." Be specific as to which user (e.g. patient, provider, admin, etc.)
      2. Include the Why! Instead of saying "so that I can use the app," say "so that I can follow up on my treatment plan"
      3. Keep it small. Each story should be able to be completed in 1 sprint.

    Remember the INVEST criteria:
      1. Independent: If stories rely on each other, consider combining them or re-sequencing
      2. Negotiable: If the story is overly detailed with technical specs or UI mockups, it’s not negotiable
      3. Valuable: Ask “Who benefits from this?” and “How does this help the user or business?”
      4. Estimable: You must be able to roughly estimate the effort or complexity of the story.
      5. Small: Small stories reduce risk, make progress visible, and give fast feedback.
      6. Testable: Vague stories like “Improve performance” or “Make it look better” are hard to test.

    Epic Title User Story Acceptance Criteria
    Virtual Consultations US001: As a patient, I want to join a video call with my doctor via my computer, so that I can receive care from home. Patient can initiate video call from appointment page 5 minutes prior to scheduled time; Provider is notified when patient has joined, Encrypted call

  3. Now we're ready to prioritize! Create a backlog using the user stories above - breaking them down further as needed.

    The Backlog:

    Hints for Backlog Creation and Prioritization:
      1. Start with the MVP in mind.
        What must be done to be ready for the 6-week demo?

      2. Your backlog should include more than just user stories.
        Make sure to include technical tasks, compliance tasks, accessibility reviews, etc. Just make sure it's trackable!

      3. Use relative sizing and don't aim for perfection.
        Estimation helps with sprint planning and capacity planning, not forecasting down to the hour.
